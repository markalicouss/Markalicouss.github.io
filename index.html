How to Install a Lift Kit

Installing a lift kit is a rewarding way to give your vehicle more clearance for offroad adventures. First, ensure you have all necessary tools, including jacks, stands, sockets, and wrenches. Begin by raising your vehicle using a jack and securing it on jack stands, making sure it’s stable. Remove the wheels to gain access to the suspension. Next, detach the old suspension components like shocks and struts. Once these are removed, install the new lift kit components according to the manufacturer's instructions, which may include new shocks, coil springs, or spacers. After everything is secured, reinstall the wheels and carefully lower the vehicle to the ground. Ensure that all parts are properly torqued to specification and get an alignment afterward for optimal performance and safety.
